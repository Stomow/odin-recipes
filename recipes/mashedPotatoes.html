Mashed Potatoes

Thank you to chatgpt otherwise known as OpenAI for the recipes

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds (about 1 kg) potatoes (Yukon Gold or Russet potatoes work best)
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • ½ cup whole milk or heavy cream (adjust according to preference)
  • Salt to taste
  • Black pepper to taste
  • Optional: Garlic, herbs (like chives or parsley), sour cream, or cream cheese
    for extra flavor

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Potatoes

    1. Peel the Potatoes: Peel the potatoes using a vegetable peeler. If you prefer
      a rustic mash with some texture, you can leave the skins on.
    2. Cut the Potatoes: Cut the potatoes into evenly sized chunks, about 1-2 inches.
      This helps them cook evenly.
    3. Rinse the Potatoes: Rinse the potato chunks under cold water to remove excess starch.

  2. Cook the Potatoes

    1. Boil the Potatoes: Place the potato chunks in a large pot and cover them with cold
      water. Add a generous pinch of salt to the water. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat
    2. Cook Until Tender: Reduce the heat to a simmer and cook the potatoes for 15-20 minutes,
      or until they are tender and easily pierced with a fork.
    3. Drain the Potatoes: Once cooked, drain the potatoes in a colander. Let them sit for a minute
      to steam off any excess water.

  3. Mash the Potatoes

    1. Heat the Milk/Butter: While the potatoes are draining, heat the milk (or cream) and butter in a
      small saucepan until the butter is melted and the mixture is warm. This helps keep the potatoes
      warm and makes them creamier.
    2. Mash the Potatoes: Return the drained potatoes to the pot or a large mixing bowl. Use a potato masher,
      ricer, or even a fork to mash the potatoes to your desired consistency.
    3. Add the Milk/Butter Mixture: Gradually add the warm milk and butter mixture to the mashed potatoes, stirring
      as you go. Continue to mash until the potatoes are smooth and creamy. Add more milk or cream if you prefer a
      looser consistency.
    4. Season to Taste: Season the mashed potatoes with salt and black pepper to taste. Stir well to combine.

  4. Add Optional Ingredients

    1. Garlic: For garlic mashed potatoes, add roasted or sautéed garlic during the mashing process.
    2. Herbs: Stir in fresh chopped herbs like chives, parsley, or thyme for added flavor.
    3. Creamy Additions: You can also add sour cream, cream cheese, or even a bit of grated cheese
      for extra richness and flavor.

  5. Enjoy!
